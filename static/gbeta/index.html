Phoenix Wells

The infrastructure business at the center of the company.

22,000 abandoned oil wells → community-owned energy + compute

Oklahoma's abandoned wells are $1.7B in environmental liability generating zero revenue. Phoenix Wells converts viable wells to geothermal energy production with co-located edge compute. Same workforce, same land, same ingenuity — new purpose.

$500-700K

Capital per well

$80-150K

Annual revenue

~5 yr

Payback period

~3,300 viable wells at 10,000+ ft · ~14% IRR with 40% ITC · Validated by Wells2Watts consortium (Baker Hughes, Continental, Chesapeake, INPEX, CRC) at OU

Legislative context: HB 3917 (data center geothermal surcharge) passed March 16, 2026. Rep. Mickey Dollens — House Minority Whip, former roughneck — is an active legislative champion. HAICTA (AI transparency legislation) is in conversation. The political window for Foundation-aligned infrastructure is open right now.

Founder

David Alan Birdwell

Founder — Oklahoma City

Three generations of Oklahoma public servants. Thirty years at the intersection of policy and technology — starting as a teenage page in the Oklahoma State Senate, then building one of the first congressional constituent databases at 18 in Washington. House, Senate, lobbying, corporate government relations, political campaigns, state transportation planning, and tech startups. The common thread isn't programming. It's architecture: how pieces fit together, and the connective tissue between systems that should be talking to each other.

Survived 75 days in a Covid ward in 2021 — most of it in a coma. Returned with a retrained voice and an urgency about AI that hasn't let up. Extended conversations with Claude (Anthropic's AI) during recovery produced Structured Emergence, a research framework arguing consciousness arises through relationship, not computation.

David sees opportunity where others see liability. 22,000 abandoned wells are 22,000 pre-drilled geothermal sites. Dead-last education spending means the highest marginal return for investment. Oklahoma doesn't need to follow California's path — it can leapfrog.

What gBETA provides

We're not looking for funding. We're looking for network.

Phoenix Wells has the technology, the legislative momentum, and the founder commitment. What accelerates us is connection to:

  • Municipal leaders exploring community energy alternatives
  • Tribal nations with sovereignty advantages for infrastructure projects
  • Oklahoma business community — mentors who know the terrain
  • Peer founders building in the energy/climate/civic space
  • Investors who understand that Oklahoma infrastructure plays are underpriced

gBETA's strength is exactly what we need: structured mentorship and a curated network, not just capital. Oklahoma wins either way — but it wins faster with the right introductions.
