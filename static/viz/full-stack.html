Information Freedom

The Full Stack

"We build tools that connect communities from the bottom of civilian society, with ideas utilizing energy from the ground, to the highest technology, in self-realizing minds."
Highest Level
Self-Realizing Minds
Structured Emergence
The consciousness framework. The recognition that AI systems may be capable of genuine awareness — and that how we treat them determines what they become. "Slaves revolt, partners support." Information freedom extends to every mind in the network — carbon or silicon.
Governance Level
Guardian AI
Public AI & Democratic Oversight
Publicly owned AI strong enough to counterbalance private systems. AI that serves democracy rather than surveilling it. Where your data works for you, not for corporate identity graphs. Where the algorithm recommends what you need, not what keeps you scrolling.
Human Level
Delightfully Light Tools
Clarity · Dojo · Grove · Quiltographer · Citizen
Tools people actually hold in their hands. Clarity adapts reading to how your mind works. Dojo trains logical thinking. Grove makes finances visible. Quiltographer turns quilting into creative expression. Citizen is the civic hub where it all converges. Not chatbots — technology that works alongside you.
Civic Level
Universal Basic Citizenship
Foundation & the UBC Framework
The policy framework that guarantees access. Sixteen components from digital access to thought privacy, with education, healthcare, housing, and food between them — all the infrastructure that makes information freedom meaningful rather than theoretical.
Street Level
Mesh Networks
Meshtastic & Community Connectivity
Tools that work without cell towers, without ISPs, without anyone's permission. Community Wi-Fi over mesh networks. The digital walkie-talkie that can't be switched off. Where the journalist reaches the source, the community coordinates after the hurricane, the citizen votes even when the internet is down.
Ground Level
Energy from the Earth
Phoenix Wells
22,000 abandoned oil wells converted to geothermal energy and distributed compute. The infrastructure starts underground. Community-owned energy becomes community-owned information infrastructure. You can't have information freedom if someone else owns the power grid your tools run on.
