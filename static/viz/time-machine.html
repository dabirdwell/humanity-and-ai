What this looks like
Rosa is not one person. She's a pattern. She's a home health aide in Tulsa — she cares for elderly people so they can stay out of nursing facilities. The work is essential. It pays $11.50/hr. The cheapest one-bedroom near her clients rents for $950 a month. She works five days a week. After rent, sleep, commute, and the basics of keeping herself alive, she has about 18 hours a week to herself. That's two and a half hours a day. Not enough to recover. Not enough to dream. Barely enough to exist.

Her counterpart in Hamburg — same job title, same hours — has 44 hours of free time a week. Six and a half hours a day. Not because she works harder. Not because she's smarter. Because the system she was born into decided to leave her more of her own life.

The difference between Rosa and her Hamburg counterpart is not talent or effort. It is policy.

Housing is Component 14 of the Foundation framework. The gap you just saw is a choice, not an inevitability.
