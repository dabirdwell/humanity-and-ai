How we rate

Verified — the claim was demonstrably accurate and supported by independent evidence at time of publication.

Supported — the claim is directionally accurate but stronger than the evidence strictly warrants.

Plausible — the claim is reasonable but not yet testable; time will tell.

Overstated — the claim exceeded what the evidence supported in a meaningful way.

False — the claim was demonstrably wrong at time of publication.

Source reliability ratings are relative to each source's own track record, not an absolute standard. "Uncharacteristic" means a source that usually does better missed here. "Consistent pattern" means the overstating is the track record.
