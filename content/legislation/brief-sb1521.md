> **Correction, July 9, 2026.** An earlier version of this brief reported that SB 1521 was enacted before the 2026 session ended. That was wrong. The bill passed both chambers unanimously, in differing versions, but the Legislature adjourned sine die on May 14, 2026, before the Senate took up the House amendments. SB 1521 died on the concurrence calendar and is not law. This brief has been rewritten to reflect the [official record](https://legiscan.com/OK/bill/SB1521/2026).

**Minor Protection from AI (Conversational AI Services)**
Author: Sen. Hamilton · House principal author: Rep. Maynard · Senate: 43-0, March 23, 2026 · House, as amended: 90-0, April 28, 2026 · Died at sine die, May 14, 2026, awaiting Senate concurrence

## What the bill would have done

SB 1521, in the version both chambers last voted on, would have regulated operators of conversational AI services that interact with minors. It asked two things of those operators: provide parental management tools for minor accounts, and ensure the AI directs users who express self-harm or suicidal thoughts to appropriate crisis services.

## How it died

The bill traveled a long way from its introduced form, which had centered on age verification, ID checks, account freezes, and civil penalties up to $100,000 per violation. The Senate floor substitute narrowed it, and the House committee substitute settled it on the parental-controls-and-crisis-routing approach. The House passed that version 90-0 on April 28 and returned it to the Senate on April 29.

The Legislature then adjourned sine die on May 14, two weeks ahead of the usual late-May close, and the Senate concurrence vote never happened. Not one legislator voted against this bill in either chamber, and it still died. The constraint was the calendar, not consensus. Because 2026 was the second session of the 60th Legislature, the bill does not carry over; a successor would have to be refiled in 2027.

## What did pass

Two AI measures became Oklahoma law in 2026: SB 1734, the Responsible Technology in Schools Act, signed May 13, and HB 3244, which adds artificial intelligence to the aggravated identity theft statute, law without signature on May 7. The House companion to this bill, HB 3544, also died at adjournment.

## What operators should take from it

There is no SB 1521 compliance obligation. Nothing in this bill binds anyone. But the design signal is worth reading: both chambers of a state legislature endorsed, without a single dissenting vote, parental management tools and crisis routing as the baseline for minor-facing conversational AI. An operator building to that standard now is building for where the next Oklahoma session, and a growing number of other states, are already headed.
