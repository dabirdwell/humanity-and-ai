## Guest Wishlist

The Humanity and AI Show is looking for guests who are building, governing, teaching, or making things at the intersection of AI and real communities. We're less interested in predictions and more interested in practice.

This is a wishlist by category — the kinds of people and perspectives we want on the show.

---

### AI Researchers Working on Public Benefit

People doing AI research outside the profit-maximization lane. Researchers focused on:
- AI safety and alignment with a governance lens
- Open-source AI infrastructure
- AI fairness, bias auditing, and accountability frameworks
- Human-AI collaboration models (not replacement models)
- AI applications in healthcare, education, or public services

**What we'd ask:** What does your research look like when it leaves the lab? Who benefits and who decides?

---

### State and Local Policymakers

Legislators, city council members, county officials, and agency directors — especially in Oklahoma but not exclusively. People working on:
- AI policy at the state or municipal level
- Workforce development and retraining programs
- Safety net modernization
- Public broadband and digital infrastructure
- Education policy in the age of AI

**What we'd ask:** What does the AI transition look like from a statehouse or city hall? What tools would actually help you govern this?

---

### Craft Makers and Small Manufacturers

People who make physical things — woodworkers, ceramicists, metalworkers, textile artists, small-batch manufacturers. People exploring:
- AI-assisted design tools in craft practice
- CNC, generative design, or parametric modeling in workshops
- The economics of handmade in an AI-accelerated market
- Preserving craft knowledge and apprenticeship in a changing economy

**What we'd ask:** How is AI showing up in your shop? What would you want it to do that it can't yet?

---

### Educators and School Leaders

Teachers, principals, superintendents, curriculum designers, and education researchers. People grappling with:
- AI in the classroom — policies, tools, and pedagogy
- Student use of AI for learning (not just cheating concerns)
- Teacher workload and AI-assisted planning
- Rural and under-resourced school challenges
- Rethinking assessment in a world where AI can write essays

**What we'd ask:** What are your students actually doing with AI right now? What do you wish you had?

---

### Community Organizers and Civic Technologists

People building tools and movements for community benefit:
- Civic tech developers building public-interest software
- Community organizers using data and technology for advocacy
- Mutual aid network coordinators
- Public librarians and information access advocates
- Digital equity and broadband access organizers

**What we'd ask:** What does your community need from the AI transition? What are you building to make sure they get it?

---

### Healthcare Workers and Public Health Leaders

Frontline healthcare providers and public health administrators:
- Rural healthcare providers navigating AI diagnostic tools
- Mental health professionals exploring AI-assisted care
- Public health officials working on data infrastructure
- Community health workers and patient advocates
- Health equity researchers

**What we'd ask:** Where is AI helping in your practice right now? Where is it making things harder?

---

### Cooperative and Alternative Economy Builders

People working on economic models beyond extractive capitalism:
- Worker cooperative founders and members
- Community land trust organizers
- Public banking advocates
- Timebanking and mutual credit practitioners
- Platform cooperative developers

**What we'd ask:** What does an economy look like where AI serves workers and communities instead of replacing them?

---

### Oklahoma Voices

People rooted in Oklahoma who bring specific local knowledge:
- Tribal nation leaders and technologists
- Oklahoma farmers and ranchers navigating precision agriculture
- Energy sector workers in transition
- Oklahoma artists, musicians, and cultural workers
- Small business owners in rural Oklahoma communities

**What we'd ask:** What does the AI transition look like from Oklahoma specifically? What's different here?

---

## How We Choose Guests

We're not booking for clout. We're booking for signal. The ideal guest:

- Is **doing the work**, not just commenting on it
- Can speak with **specificity** about their domain
- Is willing to talk about **what's hard**, not just what's working
- Brings a perspective the audience **wouldn't hear** on a typical tech podcast
- Is comfortable with honest, unrehearsed conversation

If you know someone who fits — or if you're that someone — reach out through the [Stream](/) or [About](/about/) page.
