America is in a mental health crisis that predates AI and will be dramatically worsened by it. Depression, anxiety, substance abuse, and suicide rates have been climbing for decades. The treatment infrastructure is catastrophically inadequate — long wait times, high costs, geographic deserts, and a system that treats mental health as a luxury rather than a medical necessity.

Now add the AI transition: mass economic disruption, accelerating uncertainty, the disorientation of watching machines do what you trained years to do, the loneliness epidemic amplified by algorithmic social media. The mental health demands of the next decade will dwarf anything we've seen. And we're not remotely ready.

## Why This Is Separate from Healthcare

UBC has both a mental health component and a national healthcare component because mental health in America is systematically treated as secondary. Insurance parity laws exist on paper and fail in practice. Therapy is out of reach for most working people. Addiction treatment is underfunded, stigmatized, and often criminalized rather than medicalized.

Breaking mental health out as its own component is a design statement: this isn't a subset of healthcare. It's a pillar of citizenship. A society that expects its citizens to navigate unprecedented change without providing psychological support is setting them up to fail.

## The Addiction Dimension

Addiction is a public health issue, not a criminal justice issue. Every dollar spent on criminalization rather than treatment is a dollar wasted and a life damaged. UBC's framework includes comprehensive addiction treatment as a citizenship right — accessible, destigmatized, evidence-based, and integrated with the broader mental health infrastructure.

This matters for the AI transition specifically because economic displacement and social disruption are known accelerators of substance abuse. The opioid crisis in deindustrialized America was a preview. Without proactive investment in addiction support, the AI transition will produce its own wave.

## AI-Assisted Mental Health

AI can extend the reach of mental health services — chatbots for triage, therapeutic apps for ongoing support, pattern detection for early intervention. But AI cannot replace human connection in healing. The model is augmentation, not replacement: AI handles scale and access while human practitioners provide the relational depth that actually heals.

## What We Need

Therapists, addiction counselors, people in recovery, public health researchers, and especially people who've tried to access mental health care and been failed by the system — your experience is the data we need. What's broken? What works? What would you build if you could start from scratch?
