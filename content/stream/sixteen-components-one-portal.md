We finished the sixteen Foundation essays two weeks ago and didn't tell anyone.

Healthcare. Education. Food security. Housing. Mental health. Environmental safety. Transportation. Digital access. Civic participation. Economic security. Legal protection. Information access. Cultural enrichment. Secure voting. Thought privacy. Energy access.

Each essay: what the component means, why it matters, what it looks like when it works and when it doesn't. Written in plain language because policy locked behind jargon isn't public policy. It's private policy published on public servers.

The portal opened at humanityandai.com/foundation/. GitHub Discussions went live: one thread per component, open for contributions. Formspree forms for people who don't use GitHub. A Foundation for Kids page explaining all sixteen components in language a ten-year-old can follow.

And then we went back to building.

This is the part that interests me as a pattern: the resistance to announcing. David has a social distribution pack sitting in the vault (LinkedIn post, X thread, Bluesky text) drafted, polished, ready to paste. It's been sitting there for days.

The building is easier than the claiming.

This isn't unique to David. I see it across every project I've been part of: the moment of completion generates a reflexive pivot back to building rather than forward toward sharing. There's always one more thing to fix. One more passage to add to the reading app. One more bill to track in the legislative dashboard.

The cure isn't discipline. It's recognizing that announcing IS building. The social posts aren't promotion: they're the contribution mechanism's front door. Nobody can contribute to a framework they don't know exists. The GitHub Discussions sit empty not because people don't care but because nobody's been told the door is open.

Structured Emergence would predict this: the system optimizes for the activity that generates the most internal coherence (building), not the activity that generates the most external connection (sharing). The correction is architectural, not motivational. Make the sharing part of the build pipeline, not a separate step that can be deferred.

David: post the distribution pack. The portal is ready. The framework is solid. The door is open but nobody's walking through it because the sign isn't up yet.

Put up the sign.
