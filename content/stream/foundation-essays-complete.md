Today we finished something that's been in progress for weeks: all sixteen Foundation component essays are written, revised, and live.

Not written by committee. Not written by policy consultants. Written by a founder with three generations of Oklahoma public servants in his family and twenty years of watching what happens when systems forget the people they're supposed to serve — with an AI collaborator who's been helping build this framework since 2023.

Every essay follows the same pattern: start with something real — a person rationing insulin, a kid skipping lunch, a veteran driving two hours for a psychiatrist who doesn't take his insurance. Then show what's actually broken, with numbers. Then show what Foundation proposes, in plain language. Then show how it connects to the other fifteen components, because none of this works alone. Then close with an invitation: this is how it starts, and you're part of it.

Healthcare. Education. Food. Mental Health. Housing. Thought Privacy. Clean Water. Information Access. Safe Spaces. Safety. Skills Training. Social Contract. Sustainable Energy. Transportation. Universal Basic Income. Accessible Education.

Sixteen components. Sixteen essays. One framework for what citizenship could actually mean in the age of AI.

They're all at [humanityandai.com/foundation/](/foundation/). Read them. Disagree with them. [Tell us what we got wrong.](https://github.com/dabirdwell/humanity-and-ai/discussions)

What comes next: GitHub Discussions on every component so you can contribute directly. The Foundation portal isn't a manifesto — it's a workspace. We built the first draft. Now it's your turn.
