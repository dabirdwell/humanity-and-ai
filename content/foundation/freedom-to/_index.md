The second layer of freedom is the door that opens after the floor holds. A person who is fed, housed, healthy, and safe still needs somewhere to go — education that develops judgment, not just credentials; information access that serves the citizen, not the advertiser; transportation that connects communities instead of isolating them; energy infrastructure owned by the people it powers.

Freedom To is where most progressive policy lives, and where most progressive policy stops. Open the door. Provide the opportunity. Assume the rest follows. But opportunity without structure produces the achievement culture Jennifer Wallace documents — where mattering becomes contingent on performance, and people chase attention over connection, prestige over purpose, money over meaning. The ladder is not enough if climbing it is the only way to matter.

These components are the capability layer. Each one asks not just "does the citizen have access?" but "does the access build capacity that compounds across a lifetime?"
