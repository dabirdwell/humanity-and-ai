The first layer of freedom is the oldest promise a society makes to its people: you will not starve, you will not freeze, you will not die of treatable illness, you will not live in fear. These are not aspirations. They are the minimum conditions under which a person can think clearly, plan ahead, and participate in anything beyond survival.

Every component in this generation exists because its absence makes everything else impossible. You cannot learn if you are hungry. You cannot work if you are sick. You cannot raise a child if you are unsafe. The floor is not a luxury — it is the load-bearing structure beneath every other freedom Foundation proposes.

Oklahoma spends more per capita on incarceration than on mental health, housing assistance, and job training combined. The floor is not missing because we cannot afford it. It is missing because we chose to build something else.
