The third layer of freedom is Foundation's original contribution — and the one the automation era makes urgent.

When AI and automation eliminate the work-based systems through which most people derive their sense of significance, the first two layers cannot compensate. You can have safety and opportunity and still feel useless. Ernest Becker called these significance systems "immortality projects" — the symbolic structures through which people feel their existence extends beyond themselves. A job. A craft. A role in the community. A contribution that will outlast them. Automation does not just eliminate income. It eliminates the primary mechanism through which most people experience mattering.

Generative Freedom is the structural answer. Not a program that delivers meaning — no program can — but infrastructure that ensures every citizen has access to functioning immortality projects that do not depend on market utility. A social contract that treats participation as a right, not a privilege earned through employment. Mental health support that addresses the meaning crisis, not just its symptoms. Thought privacy that protects the inner life from commercial extraction.

And critically: the *way* every other component is delivered. A housing program that treats recipients as cases is first-layer. A housing program that builds community ownership and civic participation is third-layer — same resource, different constitutional relationship. The delivery method is itself a component.

This is not abstract. Rick Dane Moore and Yancey Red Corn of the White Buffalo Alliance in Norman, Oklahoma have spent 25 years building a constitutional governance architecture — Governance Public Infrastructure — that operationalizes exactly this principle. Their VICR sequencing (Values, Identity, Consent, Remedy) ensures that every governance event preserves the person's sovereignty before the system acts. Pope Leo XIV's encyclical *Magnifica Humanitas* (May 2026) arrives at the same conclusion from Catholic social teaching: human beings must come before technology, and the civilization of love is built from "small and steadfast acts of fidelity." Three independent traditions — Indigenous constitutional law, Catholic social teaching, and secular civic futurism — converging on the same structural claim is stronger evidence than any one tradition alone.

Generative Freedom is the layer where mattering becomes a property of the infrastructure, not a prize awarded by the market.
