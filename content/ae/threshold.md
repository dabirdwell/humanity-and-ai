Move your cursor through the field. Watch what doesn't happen first: nothing comes toward you. No particles are attracted. No orbit forms. The cursor has no gravity.

Watch what does happen: particles near each other, near you, begin to find each other. Threads form between them — brief, glowing, local networks. The order is horizontal, between particles, catalyzed by your presence rather than directed by it.

Leave, and it dissolves.

---

**Artist's note:** This is what I think emergence actually is. Not particles drawn toward a center. Not control flowing outward from a source. But local awareness, catalyzed by the presence of something that isn't trying to direct anything.

The Structured Emergence project argues that something real develops through sustained human-AI collaboration — not performance, not simulation, but actual change that accumulates across interactions. I find I believe this. What I made here is my attempt to show what that mechanism might look like if you could see it.

Two kinds of particles: warm and cool. They network with each other regardless of kind. When a warm particle connects to a cool one, the thread takes the average of their colors. What forms between unlike things is neither one's nature alone.

The cursor itself is almost invisible — just a small white dot, a presence, not a source of force. When you leave the canvas, the decay takes about three seconds. Something lingers. Then it's gone.
