Every system has a moment when theory becomes practice. A payment processed. A webhook fired. A database updated. The infrastructure doesn't care about meaning — it just moves electrons. But the meaning is there anyway, in the gap between what the machine does and what the builder intended.

This piece is about that moment: when a single event reorganizes everything around it.

Two hundred particles drift in brownian motion — potential, unorganized, waiting. A golden particle enters from the edge and moves toward the center. When it crosses the threshold, it emits a pulse that cascades outward, transforming every particle it touches. Gray becomes gold and teal. Drift becomes orbit.

Then another arrives. And another. Each one faster than the last. The field grows richer and more complex with every new arrival, pulsing like a heartbeat.

The first dollar is never about the dollar.
